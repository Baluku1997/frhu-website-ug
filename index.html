Why we exist

Purpose and vision

Our purpose

Sustainable health, from the root up.

To promote and implement sustainable health interventions that empower communities to improve access to quality care and address the root causes of health disparity and poverty — ensuring long-term well-being and resilience.

Our vision

A healthier, empowered Uganda.

A healthier, poverty-free, empowered and sustainable Uganda where every individual has access to equitable health care and benefits from resilient, community-driven interventions.

What we do

Our core themes

Front Rise Health Uganda is a multidisciplinary organisation working to tackle critical health and development challenges through six connected pillars.

We raise awareness and advocate for SRHR, giving women and young people the information and space to make informed decisions about their health.

Objective — Increase access, awareness and advocacy for SRHR among women and youth in rural communities of Uganda.

We educate communities on responsible antibiotic use, hygiene and infection prevention, and build interdisciplinary collaboration against AMR.

Objective — Promote evidence-based strategies and interdisciplinary collaboration, and advocate for sustainable practices in the fight against AMR.

We work to demystify mental illness, reduce stigma, and connect people in underserved communities to early detection and appropriate care.

Objective — Improve early detection and management of mental health conditions in underserved populations through research, community intervention, capacity building and stigma reduction.

Our Research and Development Unit strengthens young scientists in southwestern Uganda, so local problems find locally-designed solutions.

Objective — Strengthen the research and innovation skills of young scientists, empowering them to develop effective local solutions and advance sustainable development.

We help communities adapt to a changing climate, safeguard environmental health, and build resilience against climate-driven health risks.

Objective — Promote climate change adaptation, environmental health and community resilience.

We strengthen community health systems and the livelihoods that sustain them, so care stays reliable, local and owned by the people it serves.

Objective — Strengthen community health systems and livelihoods.

FloodAdapt Communities Project

Youth-led flood resilience across Karusandara and Chelanga sub-counties, Kasese.

View project

Across Karusandara and Chelanga sub-counties in Kasese — one of Uganda’s most flood-prone areas — the FloodAdapt Communities Project mobilises youth and community members on flood preparedness and climate-risk awareness, trains village disaster-preparedness and management committees, and establishes bamboo nursery beds to stabilise flood-prone land — building local capacity to anticipate, withstand and recover from climate-driven flooding.
